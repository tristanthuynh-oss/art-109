Projects

Various projects i've worked on in my burgeoning career in the digital artist space.

Date-A-Neko, 2025

This was my final for my "Intro To Coding" class and while most people made a clock or something basic. One of the prompts was to make an interative art piece and so I decided that I wanted to make a game. It was supposed to be asmall demo but it ended up being a full game.

You are a funny little cat and your goal to is to score a date to a dance in small mini-game. It's nothing too crazy but for something made after only a couple of months of learning to code with no prior experience. I'd say thats pretty cool.

Fun story, when I first finished the game and the documentation something went wrong with the transfer to the live servers and I ended up losing almost all of my files related to this game with three days left before that project was due. So I ended spending 13 real hours straight, doing nothing but re-coding and remaking every asset and line which was an experience.

Play Date-A-Neko

Rat Attack, 2025

This was a recent project when I started getting into game design, my first stint using a dedicated gaming engine. That was engine was, of course, Unity.

You are the chef defend yourselves from the rats who try to eat your cheese. Your goal is to survive as much as possible before being overwhelmed by the rats

This was my first real honest game and it was rough. Often I found myself with many scripting errors with a more advanced system where I'd learned very different concepts like wiring, parent and childing, and other frustatingly neccesary concepts

Play, Rat Attack!

Self Playing Video Game, 2024

This was one of my earliest big forays into the digial art sphere, in fact this was my final for my "Intro to Digital Art" class.

This project was a lot of firsts for myself, I learned Adobe After Effects, pixel art, video editing. A true leaping point to my future career in digital art.

As someone who's always been an avid gamer, even in this facsimile of a video game it was very magical experience of simulating a rpg battle. However rudimentary it came out to be. It was the fact that I did that. I made it look like a video game with the atmosphere of someone interacting with an RPG. It was quite a inspiring experience.
